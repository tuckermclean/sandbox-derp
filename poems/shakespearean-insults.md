# Shakespearean Insults

*A curated collection of genuine insults from the Bard's own pen — because nobody could wound with words quite like William Shakespeare.*

---

## The Classics

> **"Thou art a boil, a plague sore, an embossed carbuncle in my corrupted blood."**
> — *King Lear*, Act II, Scene 4

> **"Thou poisonous bunch-backed toad!"**
> — *Richard III*, Act I, Scene 3

> **"Away, you three-inch fool!"**
> — *The Taming of the Shrew*, Act III, Scene 3

> **"I am sick when I do look on thee."**
> — *A Midsummer Night's Dream*, Act II, Scene 1

> **"Thou art as loathsome as a toad."**
> — *Troilus and Cressida*, Act III, Scene 2

---

## The Eloquently Dismissive

> **"The rankest compound of villainous smell that ever offended nostril."**
> — *The Merry Wives of Windsor*, Act III, Scene 5

> **"Thou art essentially made without seeming so."**
> — *Measure for Measure*, Act II, Scene 4

> **"More of your conversation would infect my brain."**
> — *Coriolanus*, Act II, Scene 1

> **"Thou hast no more brain than I have in mine elbows."**
> — *Troilus and Cressida*, Act II, Scene 1

> **"Would thou wert clean enough to spit upon."**
> — *Timon of Athens*, Act IV, Scene 3

---

## The Truly Devastating

> **"Thou art the son and heir of a mongrel bitch."**
> — *King Lear*, Act II, Scene 2

> **"I do desire we may be better strangers."**
> — *As You Like It*, Act III, Scene 2

> **"Thou damned and luxurious mountain goat."**
> — *Henry V*, Act IV, Scene 4

> **"There's no more faith in thee than in a stewed prune."**
> — *Henry IV, Part 2*, Act III, Scene 2

> **"Go, prick thy face and over-red thy fear, thou lily-livered boy."**
> — *Macbeth*, Act V, Scene 3

---

## The Philosophical Put-Downs

> **"His brain is as dry as the remainder biscuit after voyage."**
> — *As You Like It*, Act II, Scene 7

> **"Thou art a flesh-monger, a fool and a coward."**
> — *Measure for Measure*, Act V, Scene 1

> **"Away, you moldy rogue, away!"**
> — *Henry IV, Part 2*, Act II, Scene 4

> **"Thou art as fat as butter."**
> — *Henry IV, Part 1*, Act II, Scene 4

> **"The tartness of his face sours ripe grapes."**
> — *Coriolanus*, Act V, Scene 4
